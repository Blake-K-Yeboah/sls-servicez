What is your hourly rate?

We donât charge per hour; we only charge a fixed rate.

A fixed rate involves setting a fixed price for a service or project, rather than billing clients for the number of hours worked. This approach provides several advantages for our clients, such as greater predictability and transparency in pricing, as well as more efficient and effective service delivery.

There are several reasons why a fixed rate can be a better option than an hourly rate. First, it provides clients with greater certainty and predictability about the cost of a project. When clients know exactly how much they will be charged upfront, they can budget accordingly and avoid any surprises when the bill arrives.

Second, a fixed rate can incentivize service providers to work more efficiently and effectively. When working on an hourly basis, service providers may be inclined to take longer to complete a task in order to increase their billable hours.Â 

Finally, a fixed rate can be simpler and more transparent for both parties. Clients don't have to worry about keeping track of the hours worked or calculating the bill, and service providers don't have to spend time tracking and invoicing for hours.

When we charge a fixed rate, our clients know exactly what they will be paying for our services, without worrying about any surprises or additional charges. This helps them plan their budget and allocate resources more effectively, while also reducing the risk of any misunderstandings or disputes over billing.

In summary, while charging per hour may seem like a straightforward approach, a fixed rate provides greater clarity, predictability, and efficiency for both clients and service providers.
